*Before reading this section, I had assumed the book's editors would highlight the sharp contradictions between China and Japan at the time, with a tendency to criticize Japan. Moreover, the regions where overseas Chinese reside are not at the epicenter of Sino-Japanese tensions, allowing for research and analysis from a relatively objective and rational perspective. （Compared with the newspaper after 918 incident in Singaporean）*

*Regarding this passage, I believe there are two perspectives to consider. First, the book's audience consists of overseas Chinese, who, like the Portuguese arriving in Macau, were to some extent immigrants from other countries arriving in a new nation or region. Thus, this passage still reflects the education of overseas Chinese regarding their own identity. Second, the passage discusses the issue of treason that emerged during the War of Resistance, which was closely tied to the domestic social context in China at the time.*
